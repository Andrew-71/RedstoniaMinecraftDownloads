Downloading Litematica

  1. First, you'll want to have the Fabric mod loader installed.
  2. You also need to have the Fabric API (just put it into your mods folder). Fabric API is essential for the majority of Fabric mods to work.
  3. You also need the MaLiLib libary mod for Litematica to work. (also goes in your mods folder)
  4. Lastly, you can download Litematica itself, which you can also put in your mods folder.

Using Litematica with my Schematics

Of course, this is not a comprehensive guide to using Litematica in all its glory, only how to make it work with my builds. If you want more information on how to use Litematica, this video is a good starting point.
  1. Download the schematic you want to use in the dropdown above this one (called Farm Schematics).
  2. Put the schematic file into your "schematics" folder. This can be found in your .minecraft folder along with all your other Minecraft data such as worlds, mods, etc. Litematica will now be able to access this schematic so you can use it in your worlds.
  3. Once you open up your world, press M. This will bring up the Litematica menu.
  4. Select "Load Schematics." This should bring up a list of all the schematic files in your schematics folder. Just load up the one you downloaded and a blueprint should appear in front of you.
  5. Exit out of the menu.
  6. Hold a stick in your hand. You should notice some text in the corner of your screen.
  7. Make sure it's set to "Schematic Placement" by holding Ctrl and using your scroll wheel. (you should see it change in the text in the corner)
  8. Left-click to position one of the corners of the schematic at the desired location.
  9. You can use Alt while scrolling to position it in the exact spot you want it. (to change the direction you're moving it in, just face a different direction)
  10. Now that your schematic is in place, you can just place all the blocks as shown.
  11. Once that's done, you can just go back into your Litematica menu (press M) and go into "Schematic Placements", and press "Unload" on the schematic you have loaded to remove the blueprint.
  12. Refer to the video of the farm to find out where to AFK and what to do.
  13. All done!
